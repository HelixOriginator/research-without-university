# Research Without a University

## How One Unaffiliated Indian Researcher Built a Global DOI-Linked Research Ecosystem

This repository serves as the public media resource for the story behind an independent research ecosystem built outside traditional academic institutions.

It documents the work of Kallol Chakrabarti, an unaffiliated independent researcher whose research outputs span public policy, governance, AI governance, digital public infrastructure, open knowledge systems, and citizen-led scholarship.

Unlike conventional research programs supported by universities, laboratories, grants, or think tanks, this ecosystem was developed through open-access infrastructure and publicly verifiable research repositories.

---

## Why This Repository Exists

For generations, the production of research has largely been associated with universities, government institutions, and corporate laboratories.

The growth of open-access repositories, DOI infrastructure, digital publishing systems, and global indexing platforms has created new possibilities.

This repository explores a simple question:

> What happens when an individual researcher builds a large-scale, publicly verifiable research ecosystem without institutional affiliation?

## The Guinness World Records Story

On 20 April 2026, a proposal was submitted to Guinness World Records seeking recognition of a measurable category related to unaffiliated independent research output.

The proposal is currently under review.

Important clarification:

* No Guinness World Record is claimed.
* No Guinness World Record has been awarded.
* The category proposal remains under review.

The purpose of the proposal is to explore whether independent scholarly output can be recognized through objective and verifiable benchmarks.

## Evidence and Verification

The research ecosystem is built around publicly documented outputs.

Verification mechanisms include:

* DOI registration systems
* Open-access repositories
* Public indexing services
* Citation tracking systems
* Independent archival infrastructure

These systems allow third parties to verify authorship, publication dates, and document availability without relying on private claims.
